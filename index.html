Lyra (Ruoyu) Lan

Greetings! My name is Lyra. I'm a research assistant in the Laboratory for Aviation and Environment, part of MIT's Department of Aeronautics and Astronautics. I got my masters from MIT in 2020 in Building Technology and Computational Science. My graduate study was supported by MIT Tata Fellowship, with a focus on mitigating the air quality and health impacts of anthropogenic emissions using global-scale numerical models of the atmosphere (specifically the NASA supported GEOS-Chem and its Adjoint) on cloud computing plaforms.

I'm broadly interested in computational science and engineering, and application of machine learning methods to Earth observation data. I'm currently working on an ongoing project to identify plumes and smoke trails of wild fires in California in geostationary satellite imagery using deep learning methds.

I'm also a foodie! I have huge passion for cooking and visiting restaurants. One of my biggest dreams is to travel around the world and eat a lot of great food! I started making a food map of the places I visited and a food gallery since 2018. I just became a Level 6 local guide on Google in March 2021. Check my map out and recommend me your favorite food if you are interested in exploring with me!
